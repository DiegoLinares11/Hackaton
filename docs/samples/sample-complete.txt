AquaPure Ceramic Filter — Technical Documentation

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION
AquaPure is a gravity-fed ceramic water filtration system designed to remove bacteria,
protozoa, and turbidity from contaminated drinking water in low-resource households.
The system works by pouring source water into the upper chamber, where it passes
through a silver-impregnated ceramic candle (0.2 micron pore size) into a lower
storage tank with a tap. No electricity or pressurized water supply is required.

SDG ALIGNMENT
Primary: SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation), specifically targets 6.1 (universal
access to safe drinking water) and 6.b (community participation).
Secondary: SDG 3 (Good Health) — reduces diarrheal disease in children under 5.

PRICE
Retail price: USD $32 per unit (household size, 20L capacity).
Subsidized price through NGO partners: USD $18 per unit.

TARGET USERS
Rural households in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia without piped water access.
Typical household: 5-7 members. Designed for users with low literacy — all
instructions use pictograms. Also deployed in schools and small clinics.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
Direct competitors: LifeStraw Family 2.0 (USD $35), Tulip Siphon Filter (USD $20),
Nazava (USD $28). AquaPure differentiates through longer candle life (2 years vs.
1 year) and locally-manufactured ceramic in East Africa.

COUNTRIES / REGIONS
Currently deployed in Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Tanzania, Malawi, and Bangladesh.
Pilot programs in Nepal and Guatemala (2025).

MANUFACTURING METHOD
Ceramic candles produced in Nairobi, Kenya using locally-sourced clay and sawdust
(burnout agent for porosity), fired at 900°C in a tunnel kiln. Plastic housing
injection-molded by ISO-9001 certified partner in Mombasa. Final assembly and
QA at the Nairobi facility. Production capacity: 12,000 units/month.

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
Ceramic formulation: trade secret, held by AquaPure Ltd. Housing design: open-source
under CERN OHL v2. No patents filed. Technical drawings available on GitHub.

USER PROVISION MODEL
Three channels: (1) Direct sales to households via village-level entrepreneurs (VLEs)
trained by AquaPure; (2) Bulk sales to NGOs for distribution programs; (3) Government
procurement for school and clinic programs.

DISTRIBUTIONS TO DATE
As of Q1 2026: 184,000 units distributed across 6 countries. Estimated users reached:
1.1 million. Replacement candle sales: 42,000 units (indicator of continued use).

DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS
Total capacity: 20 liters (2x 10L chambers). Footprint: 30 cm diameter x 55 cm height.
Weight empty: 3.2 kg. Ceramic candle: 25 cm x 5 cm, 0.2 micron nominal pore size,
silver-impregnated. Housing: food-grade HDPE. Tap: brass with ceramic valve.

PERFORMANCE PARAMETERS
Flow rate: 1.5 L/hour at full chamber. Bacteria removal: >99.99% (log 4) for
E. coli, verified per NSF/ANSI P231. Turbidity removal: reduces 50 NTU input to
<1 NTU output. Candle lifespan: ~10,000 liters or 24 months.

TECHNICAL SUPPORT
Village-level entrepreneurs trained in installation, cleaning, and candle
replacement. 24-month warranty on housing, 12-month on tap. WhatsApp support line
in English, Swahili, Luganda, and Bengali. User manual in 8 languages.

REPLACEMENT COMPONENTS
Ceramic candle (USD $8, replace every 24 months). Tap assembly (USD $3, replace
as needed). Available through the same VLE network.

LIFECYCLE
Expected housing lifespan: 10 years. Ceramic candle: 2 years or 10,000 L.
End-of-life: HDPE housing is recyclable; ceramic candle is inert and can be
crushed for use as aggregate. No hazardous materials.

SAFETY
No known hazards during normal use. Silver impregnation is below WHO limits
(<0.1 mg/L leaching). Ceramic is food-grade. Housing contains no BPA. Risk:
if the candle cracks, filtration is compromised — users are trained to inspect
monthly. No risk of backflow contamination (air gap design).

EVALUATION METHODS
Laboratory testing: NSF/ANSI P231 protocol at accredited lab (SGS, Nairobi),
Q3 2021. Field evaluation: 18-month randomized controlled trial with 600
households in Kisumu, Kenya (2022-2023) in partnership with LSHTM. Outcomes
measured: diarrheal incidence (reduced 38%, p<0.01), candle lifespan under
field conditions, user compliance. Ongoing: continuous monitoring via VLE
reports and biannual household surveys.
